# User Interviews - Subscription Fatigue Study (Peer Group)

## Interview 1: MAYUR AKANIKAR, 3rd Year CS Student
- **Tools:** ChatGPT Plus, Cursor.
- **Quote:** "I pay $20 for ChatGPT and another $20 for Cursor. Honestly, I only use Cursor's 'Composer' now. I'm basically paying $40/month for the same GPT-4o model twice."
- **Insight:** Significant redundancy exists at the entry-level; users are double-paying for the same underlying LLM models.

## Interview 2: DYANESH PATIL, Engineering Intern
- **Tools:** GitHub Copilot, Claude Pro.
- **Quote:** "My internship team all have individual Pro accounts. The company just reimburses us individually. It's a mess to track, and there's no way to share specialized prompts or project contexts."
- **Insight:** Startups often scale via individual "Pro" accounts before moving to Enterprise, creating massive, unorganized "Shadow Spend."

## Interview 3 SUJIT KOLTE, Student Freelancer
- **Tools:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (Free), Gemini Advanced.
- **Quote:** "As a student, $20/month is a significant part of my budget. I often forget to cancel a tool after a project ends, so I end up paying for 'ghost seats' I'm not even using."
- **Insight:** "Subscription Inertia" is a major cost driver. Users need an automated way to flag underutilized tools.
